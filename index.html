Biography

I have been a chef for the last 17 years and the last 6 I worked at Google running some of their Cafes. It was a great and rewarding career, but I'm ready to make the big change to tech. I've always been interested in tech, just never made it a part of my career path. I grew up in New York, but lived in a bunch of other places before that and then moved to Boston where I met my future wife. We moved across the country in our old Subaru, piled high with our effects, so that she could go to grad school at UW. We settled into living in Fremont and eventually made our way to West Seattle during the pandemic. I recently switched into customer support for a tech company so that I can get my foot in the door and break into the world of software development.

My career has been predominantly in Food Service. I worked for Compassgroup USA which services lots of high end corporations and educational systems for food service. I worked as a line cook and lead for several law firms before moving to Washington and starting at google as the night lead. I dabbled in front of the house and back of the house until I took up a Sous Chef role which is really a general manager for a single cafe. I worked as the leader of several cafes and ran teams of up to 30. I was our client engagement champion frequently discussing customer engagement at public speaking events. During the pandemic I learned a lot about content creation, learning & development, and presented this information to hundreds of employees across several states.

After that I left work at a school as an interim job while I worked towards becoming a developer. A few months later I got a job as a customer support/it specialist for MarginEdge, a food service tech company. It's extremely helpful to have one foot in the door with tech and to get a chance to use things like SQL in my job gives me a taste of the dev life.
